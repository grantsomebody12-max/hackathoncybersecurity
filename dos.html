Denial-of-Service (DoS)

A Denial-of-Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack is designed to shut down a machine or network, making it inaccessible to its intended users. It essentially creates a massive digital traffic jam on the target server.

How it happens:

  • Traffic Flooding: Attackers use botnets (networks of compromised computers/IoT devices) to send an overwhelming amount of junk traffic to a target server, exceeding its capacity to respond to legitimate requests.
  • Application Layer Attacks: Instead of raw traffic volume, the attacker sends specific, complex requests that tie up the server's processing power, causing the application to freeze or crash.

Solutions:

Defending against DoS attacks requires robust network-level protections. Implement Web Application Firewalls (WAF) and Anti-DDoS services (like Cloudflare), which can absorb massive traffic spikes and filter out malicious requests before they hit your main server. Overprovisioning your network bandwidth and implementing rate limiting can also help mitigate smaller attacks.
