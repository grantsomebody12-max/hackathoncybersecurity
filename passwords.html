Passwords

Passwords are the keys to your digital life. A weak password can allow attackers to easily compromise your email, bank accounts, and personal data. Building strong password habits is your first and most important line of defense against cyber threats.

Common Password Mistakes:

  • Password Reuse: Using the same password across multiple sites means one data breach compromises all your accounts.
  • Personal Information: Using pet names, birthdays, or family members' names that can be easily found on your social media.
  • Short Passwords: Passwords under 8 characters can be brute-forced (guessed by a computer) almost instantly.
  • Predictable Patterns: Using sequential numbers (123456) or keyboard walks (qwerty).

How to stay secure:

Length is strength! Use "passphrases" (like "BlueElephantDancingSlowly") which are long but easy to remember. Better yet, use a reputable Password Manager to generate and store unique, complex passwords for every site. Finally, always enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) whenever possible.
