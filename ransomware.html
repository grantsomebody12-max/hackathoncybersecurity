Ransomware

Ransomware is a devastating form of malware that encrypts a victim's files, making them completely inaccessible. The attacker then demands a ransom payment—usually in cryptocurrency like Bitcoin—in exchange for the decryption key needed to restore access.

Solutions:

The absolute best defense against ransomware is maintaining regular, disconnected (offline) backups of your important files. If you are attacked, you can wipe the system and restore from the backup without paying. Additionally, keep your operating system updated, use reputable endpoint security software, and never open suspicious email attachments.
