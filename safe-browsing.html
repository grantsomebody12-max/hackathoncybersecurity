Safe Browsing

Browsing the web can be like walking through a bustling city. While most places are safe, there are dark alleys and pickpockets you need to avoid. Safe browsing habits protect you from drive-by downloads, tracking, and malicious sites.

Key Practices:

Look for HTTPS: Always ensure the websites you visit use 'HTTPS' (look for the padlock icon next to the URL), which means your connection to the site is encrypted.
Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Public networks at cafes or airports can be easily intercepted. Use a Virtual Private Network (VPN) if you must check your bank account or send private info.
Use Ad Blockers: Malicious ads (malvertising) can install malware on your computer without you even clicking them. An ad blocker significantly reduces this risk.
Keep Your Browser Updated: Software updates patch vulnerabilities that hackers use to exploit your system. Never ignore an update prompt.
