Ransomware

Malware that encrypts a victim's files, making them inaccessible. The attacker then demands a ransom payment, often in cryptocurrency, in exchange for the decryption key.


Impact: Can shut down entire businesses, hospitals, and city governments, leading to massive financial loss and disruption of critical services.

Denial-of-Service (DoS)

An attack designed to make a website or online service unavailable to its intended users. This is achieved by overwhelming the target with a flood of internet traffic.


Impact: Prevents legitimate users from accessing services, causing loss of revenue and reputational damage for online businesses.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)

An attacker secretly intercepts and relays communication between two parties who believe they are communicating directly with each other. This allows the attacker to eavesdrop and alter conversations.


Impact: Can be used to steal login credentials and personal information on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks.

Zero-Day Exploit

A cyber attack that occurs on the same day a weakness is discovered in a piece of software. Because the developer has had "zero days" to fix it, these attacks are highly effective.


Impact: Can lead to widespread, severe breaches before a patch is available, as seen in major software vulnerabilities like Log4j.
